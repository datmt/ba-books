== Preface

Welcome to your practical guide to becoming an effective Business Analyst.

This book is different from traditional BA textbooks. Instead of abstract theory, you'll find:

* Real examples from actual projects
* Step-by-step guides for every technique
* Hands-on exercises you can complete today
* Honest advice about what really works

Whether you're just starting your BA career or looking to solidify your foundational skills, this book will guide you through 48 comprehensive chapters organized into 12 practical parts.

=== How to Use This Book

Each chapter follows a consistent structure:

* **At a Glance**: Quick overview of what you'll learn
* **Core Concepts**: Theory explained in plain language
* **Real Examples**: Extended scenarios showing techniques in action
* **Step-by-Step Guides**: Concrete instructions you can follow
* **Practical Exercises**: Hands-on practice with clear success criteria
* **Common Pitfalls**: What to avoid and how to recover
* **Self-Check**: Questions to verify your understanding

=== About the 30-Week Learning Path

This book is designed to be completed over 30 weeks at 6-10 hours per week:

* Weeks 1-10: Foundation (individual skills)
* Weeks 11-20: Integration (combining skills)
* Weeks 21-30: Application (realistic projects)

You'll complete three major capstone projects that demonstrate your growing mastery.

[glossary]
== Glossary

[glossary]
Acceptance Criteria:: Conditions that must be met for a requirement to be considered complete and acceptable to stakeholders.

Business Analyst:: A professional who analyzes business needs, identifies solutions, and facilitates communication between stakeholders and technical teams.

BABOK:: Business Analysis Body of Knowledge - the guide published by IIBA defining BA standards and practices.

Elicitation:: The process of gathering requirements and information from stakeholders.

Functional Requirement:: A requirement that specifies what a system must do - its behaviors and functions.

Non-Functional Requirement:: A requirement that specifies how a system performs - quality attributes like performance, security, usability.

Stakeholder:: Any person, group, or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by a solution.

User Story:: A short, simple description of a feature told from the perspective of the person who desires the new capability.
